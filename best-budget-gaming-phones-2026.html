Best Budget Gaming Phones in 2026 (Under €350)

Most phones marketed as "gaming phones" charge a premium for RGB lighting and shoulder triggers you'll use twice. What actually matters for a smooth mobile session is three things: a chipset that holds its clock speed under load, a refresh rate the display can sustain, and a battery that survives a real 40-minute match without throttling. Here's how three genuinely solid budget options stack up.

Quick comparison

Model Display Battery RAM Approx. price
Poco X7 Pro 6.67" AMOLED, 120Hz 6000mAh, 90W charging 8GB ~€280–300
Samsung Galaxy A36 5G 6.7" Super AMOLED, 120Hz 5000mAh, 45W charging 8GB ~€280–300
Motorola Moto G55 5G 6.49" IPS LCD, 120Hz 5000mAh, 30W charging 8GB ~€170–230

1. Poco X7 Pro — best overall performance for the price

  Sustained performance

The X7 Pro runs on MediaTek's Dimensity 8400 Ultra, which is genuinely overpowered for this price bracket — it's the kind of chip you'd expect in a phone costing €100 more. Paired with a 6000mAh battery and 90W charging, it's built for people who play long sessions and don't want to think about the charger until bedtime. The AMOLED panel hits 120Hz, so scrolling and fast-paced games both look smooth. The tradeoff: cameras and software polish take a back seat to raw performance here, and Xiaomi's HyperOS software can feel busy compared to stock Android.

2. Samsung Galaxy A36 5G — best if you want long-term software support

  Sustained performance

The A36 doesn't out-benchmark the Poco, but it wins on two things gamers underrate: cooling and longevity. Samsung fitted it with a 15% larger vapor chamber than its predecessor specifically to manage heat during extended use, and it comes with six years of OS and security updates — most budget phones in this range get two or three. If you're the type to keep a phone for 3+ years rather than upgrading every cycle, that support window is worth more than a few extra frames per second.

3. Motorola Moto G55 5G — best if €200 is your hard ceiling

  Sustained performance

The G55 is the compromise pick, and an honest one: you still get a 120Hz display and a 5000mAh battery, but the IPS LCD panel (not AMOLED) and slower 30W charging show where Motorola cut costs to hit this price. For casual and mid-intensity games it holds up fine; for anything graphically demanding, expect more noticeable frame dips than the other two. Fine choice if this is a secondary or backup device rather than your main one.
